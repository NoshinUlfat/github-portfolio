About

Hi, I'm Noshin Ulfat, a Computer Science PhD student at the University of Texas at Dallas, where I serve as a Graduate Teaching Assistant and research in the AS²ERT Lab (AI for Safe Software Engineering and Testing).

My work lies in software engineering and software testing, with a particular focus on how large language models can help teams build more dependable software. I'm motivated by a simple conviction: software can appear correct and still fail where it matters most, and disciplined testing is what closes that gap.

Prior to my PhD, I spent nearly three years as a software development engineer at IQVIA, building production web applications with Angular and TypeScript — experience that keeps my research grounded in the realities of professional software teams. I hold a Bachelor of Science in Computer Science and Engineering from the Bangladesh University of Engineering and Technology (BUET), and I have collaborated on research with the University of Notre Dame. I also maintain a strong interest in natural language processing and the responsible use of AI.

Beyond my work, I'm an avid reader and enjoy writing short stories. I also draw in my free time; you're welcome to explore some of my artwork on the Arts page.

Research
